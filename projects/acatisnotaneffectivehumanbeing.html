Drawing a webcomic about a cat

During a spell of bad head-weather in late 2013, I conceived that creating a sort of philosophical comic about a cat trying to come to terms with its nature (as a cat living in a world of humans) held the key to my salvation. At the time I had a lot of ideas about topics it would explore: one's role in society, the nature of identity, and different ways of living in this world. The first attempt, to the detriment of my studies that fall, resulted in this.

The general premise was that the cat is disturbed from its slumber and pressured to find work. To provide for its owner/landlord. When this doesn't work out, and the bank forecloses on the landlord's home, they are left out on the streets. Various episodes and encounters in those alleys were to commence, wherein the cat would meet various wacky characters and ultimately learn valuable life lessons.

Eventually (early 2017), I got the style I was looking for dialed in, consolidated many of my doodles and notes into a coherent aesthetic, took a stab at continuing the story, and then gave up.
